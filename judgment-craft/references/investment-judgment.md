# Second-Level Investment Judgment

Use this adapter when judgment becomes an investment thesis. Its purpose is not to produce a clever contrarian opinion. It is to identify an edge that is more accurate than consensus, absent from the price, executable after friction, and sized so one adverse state cannot end the process.

## Core Contract

```text
Investment edge = correct × non-consensus × unpriced × executable
Position quality = edge × survival × optionality × updating discipline
```

Treat these as gates, not literal equations. If any edge term is missing, the decision is hold/pass. A good company, true story, or different opinion is not enough.

## 1. Classify The Asset Before Choosing A Style

- **Mean-reversion asset:** normalized economics remain intact and price has moved away from value. Contrarian logic may work; test balance-sheet survival and whether the cycle is temporary.
- **Paradigm-shift asset:** innovation may rewrite industry economics and reinforce the winner through scale, data, distribution, talent, or capital. Require real demand, improving unit economics, and a value-creating engine.
- **Mixed asset:** value some parts on normalized economics and others as stage-gated options. Do not let an exciting option suspend discipline for the whole company.

`Paradigm shift` is not permission to ignore price. Pair reflexivity with intrinsic-value discipline: growth earns a premium only while incremental ROIC can exceed the cost of capital.

## 2. Map Consensus, Price, And Divergence

Record five things in order:

1. the surface consensus and its strongest evidence
2. what the current price already requires about growth, margins, duration, and risk
3. the one or two variables on which your view differs
4. why your evidence, interpretation, horizon, or base rate is better
5. the outcome range if consensus is right, you are right, or both are partly wrong

Do not call a view variant perception until it is different, more accurate, and absent from price.

## 3. Use Reflexivity Without Worshipping Momentum

Price can change fundamentals through capital cost, talent, customer confidence, acquisitions, and competitor behavior. Track whether that loop strengthens real economics or only finances a narrative.

Accept **fuzzy correctness** when the crux and evidence are validating: a perfect entry is unnecessary. Preserve a price boundary and scenario range; entry flexibility is not unlimited valuation tolerance.

## 4. Price Edge Decay And Implementation

Estimate when consensus, competition, capacity, liquidity, borrow, fees, tax, access, or execution delay erases the advantage. State:

- expected edge half-life
- capacity before the trade moves or concentrates risk
- total implementation friction
- refresh signal and expiry date

Being right after the gap closes is not edge.

## 5. Translate The Thesis Into A Survivable Position

Markets are adaptive mixed worlds. A backtest, accounting identity, or repeated KPI is only a small-world slice.

- Keep diversification, liquidity, mandate, and risk limits as the robust baseline.
- Size from gap risk, tail loss, leverage, correlation, liquidity, and thesis uncertainty—not conviction alone.
- Use a small observation position only when it purchases named information. `1–2%` is an example, not a default; use less for high-tail or correlated exposure.
- Predeclare what evidence earns an add, what blocks an add, and what forces an exit.
- Never relabel a failed growth thesis as value to avoid realizing error.

## 6. Review Process Separately From P&L

Before entry, timestamp the consensus, price-implied view, crux, scenarios, sizing logic, edge half-life, and falsifier. Afterward, classify:

- **Good loss:** calibrated thesis and sizing, falsifier respected, adverse state realized.
- **Bad win:** weak or unfalsifiable thesis, excessive exposure, favorable variance rescued it.
- **Useful miss:** no or small position, but the error changes classification, evidence standards, or execution.

Missed profit is R&D tuition only when the process changes. Regret without a system update is not learning.
