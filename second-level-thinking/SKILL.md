# Non-Linear Second-Level Framework

> Howard Marks' *second-level thinking* (《投资最重要的事》) as the spine, upgraded so it does not misfire on paradigm-shift assets. Goal: stay independent of the crowd **and** independent of dogmatic valuation models.

## Core principle (Marks)

Your goal is **not to be right — it is to be *more right than the consensus*, in a way the price does not already reflect.** Being correct and in line with consensus earns nothing because it is already priced. To outperform, your thinking must be both different from and better than the crowd's.

- **First-level thinking:** "Good company → buy." "Bad outlook → sell." It needs only an opinion about the future.
- **Second-level thinking:** "Good company, but everyone knows it and the price assumes perfection." "Profits will fall, but less than feared." It reasons about reality, consensus, price, and adaptation together.

The edge lives in the gap between **what is priced** and **what will actually happen**—after costs, capacity, and the market's response.

## Step 1 — Classify the asset first (物理定律 vs 生物进化)

This gate decides what second-level thinking means for the name:

- **Mean-reversion asset** (traditional, consumer, cyclical): classic contrarian logic applies when price moves far from normalized value. Governing rule: price ultimately reverts toward value.
- **Paradigm-shift asset** (foundational innovation that rewrites industry economics): reflexivity and trend confirmation matter. Governing rule: growth can widen the moat and help finance the transition.

Do not use the label `paradigm-shift` to suspend valuation or falsification. Pair with `intrinsic-value-discipline`: reflexivity earns a premium only while real demand, unit economics, and ROIC-versus-WACC support the engine.

## Step 2 — Find the expectation gap

Interrogate the seam between crowd perception and objective reality:

- What is the surface consensus, and what does the current price already require?
- Is the market extrapolating linearly across a non-linear change, or narrating a paradigm shift around ordinary economics?
- What information, interpretation, horizon, psychology, or reflexive mechanism makes your view different and better?
- What happens to price if consensus is right, if you are right, and if both are partly wrong?

Non-consensus alone is not edge. Require all three: **different from consensus, more accurate, and not already priced.**

## Step 3 — Rebuild the valuation lens (accept fuzzy correctness)

- **Reflexivity:** price can affect fundamentals by changing capital cost, talent, customer confidence, and competitor behavior. Track whether that loop strengthens the real engine or merely finances a bubble.
- **Fuzzy correctness:** do not demand a perfect entry when the crux and trend are validating, but do not confuse entry flexibility with unlimited price tolerance.
- **Range before point estimate:** compare scenario-weighted upside and downside; keep model error visible for young, cyclical, or regime-changing assets.

## Step 4 — Translate judgment into a survivable position

Apply `judgment-craft`'s risk-bearing discipline before sizing:

1. **Classify the decision world.** Markets are mixed/reflexive large worlds; a backtest, accounting identity, or repeated KPI is only a small-world slice.
2. **Estimate edge half-life.** Name when consensus, competition, capacity, liquidity, borrow, fees, tax, access, or execution delay erases the advantage.
3. **Keep a robust baseline.** Diversification, liquidity, mandate, and risk limits remain the default; a variant position is the targeted deviation and creates its own counter-exposure.
4. **Size for survival, not excitement.** Use gap risk, tail loss, leverage, correlation, liquidity, and thesis uncertainty—not conviction or expected value alone.
5. **Buy information with optionality.** An observation position, often 1–2% and smaller for high-tail names, is justified only when it names the evidence that earns an add or forces an exit.
6. **Pre-write the break.** If the crux or reflexive loop fails, cut without relabeling the position as value. Never use other people's capital outside mandate because portfolio EV looks attractive.

## Step 5 — Review process separately from outcome

Before entry, timestamp the consensus, expectation gap, crux, range, sizing logic, edge half-life, and falsifier. Afterward, grade decision quality separately from P&L:

- **Good loss:** calibrated thesis and sizing, falsifier respected, adverse state realized.
- **Bad win:** weak or unfalsifiable thesis, excessive exposure, favorable variance rescued it.
- **Useful miss:** no position or a small position, but the error upgrades asset classification, evidence standards, or timing logic.

Treat missed profit as R&D tuition only if the process changes. Regret without a system update is not learning.

## Discipline / tests

- Type the asset before choosing contrarian versus trend-following logic.
- State consensus, what price embeds, your divergence, and why it is more accurate.
- State the edge's half-life and total implementation friction.
- Define maximum tolerable loss, observation/add/exit steps, and the falsification trigger before entry.
- Do not let a lucky P&L validate weak reasoning or an unlucky P&L invalidate a sound process.
- If there is no correct, non-consensus, unpriced, and executable edge, the decision is hold/pass.
